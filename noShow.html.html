Introduction¶

The dataset that has been selected for analysis is the No_show appointmets dataset from Kaggle. The dataset has 14 columns and 110527 rows. The data has no null value however it is a mixture of Float, Int and Objects.

This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment

Discussing the structure of the Data¶

There are 110527 records and 14 columns in the dataset.

Prima Facie observations are:¶

  1. There are 13 independent and one dependent variables in the data.
  2. There is no NAN value.
  3. Patient ID is a float.
  4. Scheduled and Appointment day are objects where they can be date and time.
  5. The column names need to be corrected of their spelling and format.
  6. There is negative age.

Data Cleaning¶

Columns are renamed appropriately. Date and Time is corrected along with the removal of absurd data from Age. Status (No-show) is changed to Integer. Age is formatted to a more believable number.

Conclusions¶

The data has been explored to find meaningful insights and correlation. The data has been reduced for the purpose of analysis, while considerable care has been taken to not remove the important aspects.

The findings suggest that more research is required into the topics discussed below. They will need statistical enquiry to validate results.

  1. Most of the patients were not diagnosed as alcoholics, Diabetic, Hypertensed or Disabled.
  2. More of the patients were Diabetic than Alcoholic.
  3. The standard Deviation for Status (no-shows) is 0.4.
  4. The average age of Patients is 37 where 25% are below the age of 18. Most of the patients lie between the age group of 20 and 60.
  5. On average, 20% of appointments were missed.
  6. There are patients with multiple appointments. Top 10 range between 88 to 55 appointments per person.
  7. No distinction could be found between Males and Females for missing appointments.
